Overview of Better Call Saul

Better Call Saul is a prequel to Breaking Bad, set in the late 2000s and early 2010s, following the transformation of Jimmy McGill (Bob Odenkirk), a struggling public defender, into the morally ambiguous lawyer Saul Goodman, who becomes Walter White’s criminal attorney in Breaking Bad. The show explores Jimmy’s personal and professional struggles, starting with his rocky relationship with his brother Chuck McGill (Michael McKean), a successful lawyer who disapproves of Jimmy’s career choice. As the series progresses, Jimmy's journey into the criminal underworld intensifies, especially after his brother’s tragic downfall. Alongside this, we see the emergence of key Breaking Bad characters, like Mike Ehrmantraut (Jonathan Banks), who works as a private investigator and fixer, and Gus Fring (Giancarlo Esposito), the infamous drug kingpin. Throughout the series, the plot delves into Jimmy's internal conflict as he grapples with his desire for legitimacy and the lure of easier, morally questionable paths to success. His transformation is slow but steady, with his persona as Saul Goodman beginning to emerge as he embraces his darker side. The series also touches on themes of family, loyalty, betrayal, and the blurred lines between good and evil. The slow-burning character development, intricate storytelling, and tension-filled moments make Better Call Saul a deeply compelling narrative that complements Breaking Bad while standing on its own.

Cast Information

  1. Bob Odenkirk as Jimmy McGill / Saul Goodman / Gene Takavic
    • A former con artist turned struggling lawyer who gradually embraces the persona of the morally dubious attorney, Saul Goodman. He later adopts the identity of Gene Takavic while hiding after the events of Breaking Bad.
  2. Rhea Seehorn as Kim Wexler
    • A highly skilled lawyer and Jimmy’s love interest. She is ambitious, independent, and struggles with ethical dilemmas throughout the series.
  3. Jonathan Banks as Mike Ehrmantraut
    • A former Philadelphia cop turned fixer and enforcer for drug cartels. He is highly skilled in surveillance, security, and problem-solving.
  4. Michael McKean as Chuck McGill
    • Jimmy’s older brother, a brilliant but mentally ill lawyer who suffers from a psychosomatic sensitivity to electricity. He deeply distrusts Jimmy and their conflict is central to the early seasons.
  5. Giancarlo Esposito as Gustavo “Gus” Fring
    • A ruthless yet composed drug kingpin who owns Los Pollos Hermanos as a front for his criminal empire. He has a deep rivalry with the Salamanca cartel.
  6. Patrick Fabian as Howard Hamlin
    • A successful lawyer and managing partner at Hamlin, Hamlin & McGill (HHM). He often finds himself caught in the McGill brothers' drama.
  7. Tony Dalton as Lalo Salamanca
    • A charming but dangerous member of the Salamanca cartel who becomes one of the main antagonists. He is intelligent, ruthless, and unpredictable.
  8. Mark Margolis as Hector Salamanca
    • A high-ranking member of the Salamanca cartel and uncle to Tuco, Lalo, and the Cousins. After a stroke, he communicates only through a bell.
  9. Michael Mando as Nacho Varga
    • A lieutenant in the Salamanca cartel who wants out of the criminal world. He is caught between Gus Fring and the Salamancas, making his situation dangerous.
